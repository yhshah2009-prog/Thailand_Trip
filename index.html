Dining Strategy for Northern & Southern Thailand
  • Hotel Restaurants: Pre-inform resort chefs about Jain requirements (email 2 weeks before). Luxury hotels can prepare authentic Jain meals.

Key Jain Dietary Points to Communicate

  • No onion (พอท - phot)
  • No garlic (กระเทียม - kratiam)
  • No root vegetables - potato, carrot, radish, ginger
  • Pure vegetarian - no eggs, no meat stock
  • Separate utensils if possible

Pro Tip: Most Thai Buddhist vegetarian (เจ - "Jay") food aligns well with Jain principles. Look for yellow "เจ" signs indicating Buddhist vegetarian restaurants.

Essential Travel Tips for Thailand

Money & Banking

  • Currency: Thai Baht (THB)
    ₹1 = ~0.42 THB (check current rates)
  • Exchange: Best rates in India before departure
  • ATMs: Widely available, charge 220 THB per transaction
  • Credit Cards: Accepted at hotels, malls, restaurants
  • Cash Needed: Markets, street food, taxis
  • Tipping: Not mandatory but appreciated (20-50 THB for good service)

Communication

  • Language: Thai (English widely spoken in tourist areas)
  • Translation App: Google Translate essential
  • SIM Card: AIS or True Mobile (~500 THB for 10 days unlimited data)
  • Airport Purchase: SIM available at arrival hall
  • WiFi: Free at all hotels, many restaurants
  • Phone: WhatsApp works perfectly

Transportation

  • Grab App: Thai version of Uber - essential download
  • Taxis: Insist on meter or use Grab
  • Tuk-tuks: Negotiate price before boarding
  • BTS/MRT: Bangkok's excellent metro system
  • Songthaews: Shared pickup trucks in Phuket (20-30 THB)
  • Airport Transfers: Pre-book for convenience

Cultural Etiquette

  • Temples: Remove shoes, cover shoulders and knees
  • Royal Family: Never disrespect the King (serious offense)
  • Buddha Images: Never climb or pose disrespectfully
  • Greeting: "Wai" (hands together, slight bow)
  • Shoes: Remove when entering homes and some shops
  • Feet: Don't point feet at people or Buddha images
  • Monks: Women should not touch monks

Family-Specific Tips

  • For Parents (65 years): Thailand is very senior-friendly. Most attractions have seating areas, clean restrooms, and locals are respectful to elderly. Don't hesitate to request assistance.
  • For Toddler: Thai people LOVE children! Expect lots of attention, smiles, and occasional gentle interactions. It's culturally normal and shows warmth.
  • Stroller Navigation: Bangkok temples can be challenging. Resort areas and malls are perfectly stroller-friendly.
  • Heat Management: Schedule outdoor activities for early morning or late afternoon. Rest during peak heat (12-3 PM).
  • Hydration: Toddlers and elderly are prone to dehydration. Carry water bottles always.
  • Flexibility: Don't overschedule. Leave room for spontaneous rest and adjustments.

    Why Add Doi Inthanon to Your Itinerary?

    Educational: Learn about different ecosystems, hill tribes, Thai royalty, conservation efforts.

    Photogenic: Some of Thailand's most beautiful landscapes - pagodas, waterfalls, cloud forests, mountain vistas.
